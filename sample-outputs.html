AI-Generated Outputs

Real outputs from Groq API (Llama 3.3 70B) on a Biology study note

✨ AI Summary

Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy through two sequential stages: light-dependent reactions in thylakoid membranes that produce ATP and NADPH, and the Calvin cycle in the stroma that uses these products to fix CO₂ into glucose. The overall equation produces one glucose molecule and six oxygen molecules for every six CO₂ molecules consumed.

📌 Key Points

  • Light reactions occur in thylakoid membranes using chlorophyll
  • Water is split during photolysis, releasing O₂ as byproduct
  • ATP and NADPH from light reactions power the Calvin cycle
  • RuBisCO fixes CO₂ into 3-carbon G3P molecules
  • C4 plants have more efficient photosynthesis in hot climates

  • Memorise all steps of the Calvin cycle with intermediates

📚 Generated Flashcards

Question

Where do the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis occur?

Answer

In the thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts. Stacked thylakoids form grana, maximising surface area for light absorption.

Question

What is photolysis and what does it produce?

Answer

Splitting of water using light: 2H₂O → 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ + O₂. Produces protons, electrons, and oxygen gas.

Question

What enzyme fixes CO₂ in the Calvin cycle?

Answer

RuBisCO — the most abundant enzyme on Earth. It catalyses the first step of carbon fixation in the stroma.

Question

How do C4 plants differ from C3 plants?

Answer

C4 produces a 4-carbon OAA as first CO₂ product. More efficient in hot climates. Examples: maize, sugarcane (C4) vs wheat, rice (C3).
